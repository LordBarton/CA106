The life of the German Dictator - Adolf Hitler



Early Life


Adolf Hitler was born on April 20 1889 in small town in Austria on the German Border. Adolf didnt succeed much in school so he dropper out and pursued a carreer as an Artist even thought he was rejected from Viennas's Academy of Fine Arts. After his parents died he moved in Vienna in 1908 where his interest in politics would began and he would develop many ideas that would evantually become the Nazi Ideology



Military Career


In 1913 when World War I broke out, Hitler volunteered in a reserve infantry regiment. He was deployed in October 1914 to Belgium where he won 2 decorations for Bravery, including the rare Iron Cross First Class which he wore until he died.
When news arrived of Germany's defeat Hitler was outraged. Hitler then came to believe that the defeat wasnt due to the Ally forces but rather to Geramny's own soldiers being insufficent and patriotic "traitors". This would then set the stage for Hilter's rise to power




The Rise of the Nazi Party


After the war, Hitler return to Munich in 1918, where he joined a small German Worker's Party who's goal was to unite the working class with a strong German nationalism. In 1920 he left the arm and was made head of the the party's propaganda efforts.


One of Hitler's great propaganda ideas was adopting a version of the swastika which was originally a sacred symbol of Hinduism,Janism and Buddhism as the party's emblem, which would later strike fear to anyone who saw it in the year to come.
At the end of 1921, Hitler was leading the gorwing Nazi Party by capitalizing on the widespread anger about the Weimar Republic and the punishing terms of the Treaty of Versailles. This then brought many former army officer to Munich which would join the Nazi Party who recruited "strong arm" squads known as the SA, which Hitler used for the security of party meetings and also to attack the party's opponents.



The Third Reich


In 1932 Hitler ran for president, he recieved 36.8 percent of the vote. With the government in chaos and 3 consecutive chancellors failing to maintain control, Hitler was named chancellor which began the Third Reich.
Then in 1934 when President Hindenburg died on August 2, military leaders aggreed to combine the presidency and chancellorship into one position, meaning Hitler was commander of all the armed forces of the Country.



The Persecution of Jews


In September of 1935, Hitler passed the Nuremberg Laws which stripped all Jews of German citizenship and barred them from marrying or ahving relations with people of German blood. Additionall Laws were passed in the coming years which took away Jew's political and civil rights.
Hitler's new government also forced newspaper's out of business and used radio and movies for propaganda to increase the Nazism Ideology, Hitler also forced all German teacher's to be a member of the party. The persecution of jews was carried by the Geheime Staatspolizei (GESTAPO), an arm of the SS the expanded during this period.
